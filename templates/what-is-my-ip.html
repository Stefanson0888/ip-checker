Understanding Your IP Address

Your IP (Internet Protocol) address is your unique identifier on the internet. When you ask "what is my IP address?", you're looking for this digital postal address that allows websites and services to communicate with your device. Whether you need to find your public IP for network troubleshooting or want to check if your VPN is working, our free IP checker tool provides instant, accurate results.

Frequently Asked Questions

An IP address is a unique numerical identifier assigned to every device connected to the internet. It consists of four numbers separated by dots (IPv4) like 192.168.1.1, or a longer format for IPv6. Your IP address allows other computers to find and communicate with your device.
Yes, you can change your IP address in several ways: restart your router (for dynamic IPs), use a VPN service, connect through a proxy server, or contact your ISP for a static IP change. VPNs are the most popular method for temporary IP changes.
Your IP address is semi-public information. Websites you visit can see it, and it reveals your approximate location and ISP. However, it doesn't directly reveal your personal identity, home address, or browsing history without additional investigation.
Most home internet connections use dynamic IP addresses, which your ISP can change periodically. This happens when you restart your router, after power outages, or based on your ISP's policies. Business connections often have static (unchanging) IP addresses.
IP geolocation is generally accurate for country and region detection (95%+ accuracy), moderately accurate for cities (60-80% accuracy), but not precise enough for exact addresses. The accuracy depends on your ISP's infrastructure and database quality.
